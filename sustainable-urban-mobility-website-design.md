## 1. Brand Identity & Color Palette

### Primary Colors
- **Cyan/Bright Blue**: #00A9E0 (UN-Habitat signature)
  - Used for CTAs, navigation, highlights
  - Represents innovation, sustainability, progress
- **Dark Navy**: #1B3A5C
  - Used for text, footers, deep backgrounds
  - Represents stability, professionalism, trust
- **White**: #FFFFFF
  - Primary background, clean spacing
- **Accent Green**: #7CB342 (for eco-friendly elements)
  - Used sparingly for sustainability messaging
  - Highlights eco-friendly vehicle options

### Secondary Colors
- Light Gray: #F5F5F5 (content backgrounds)
- Medium Gray: #757575 (secondary text)
- Yellow/Gold: #FFC107 (for urgent CTAs like "Donate")

## 2. Vehicle Categories & Imagery

### A. Public Transportation Vehicles
**Purpose**: Showcase sustainable, mass-transit solutions
- **Buses** (electric, modern fleet)
  - Angle: 3/4 view, side profile
  - Setting: Urban street, multiple passengers visible
  - Color treatment: Modern, clean livery with brand colors
- **Trams/Light Rail**
  - Angle: Overhead or street-level tracking shot
  - Setting: Modern city infrastructure
  - Emphasis: Clean energy, multiple passengers

### B. Micro-mobility & Active Transport
**Purpose**: Show first-mile/last-mile solutions
- **E-bikes & Bicycles**
  - Angle: Lifestyle shots with riders
  - Setting: Urban neighborhoods, parks, residential areas
  - Feeling: Accessible, healthy, community-focused
- **E-scooters**
  - Angle: Dynamic action shots
  - Setting: City centers, pedestrian zones
  - Feeling: Modern, convenient, young demographic

### C. Shared Mobility
**Purpose**: Demonstrate resource efficiency
- **Car-sharing vehicles** (compact electric cars)
  - Angle: 3/4 front view, parked in urban station
  - Branding: Clean, minimal corporate design
  - Color: White, silver, or electric blue
- **Delivery vehicles** (e-cargo bikes, small electric vans)
  - Angle: Action shot on city streets
  - Purpose: Show last-mile logistics solutions
  - Emphasis: Cargo capacity, zero-emissions

### D. Future/Concept Vehicles
**Purpose**: Inspire vision of sustainable cities
- **Autonomous shuttles**
  - Angle: Futuristic, clean lines
  - Setting: Smart city environments
  - Feeling: Forward-thinking, innovative
- **Electric commercial vehicles**
  - Angle: Professional, fleet-ready appearance
  - Setting: Urban delivery, logistics hubs
  - Feeling: Practical, scalable solutions

## 4. Vehicle Photography Guidelines

### Professional Standards
- **Resolution**: Minimum 2000x1200px (all hero images)
- **Format**: JPG for full-color, PNG for transparency
- **Quality**: Professional stock or commissioned photography
- **Consistency**: Unified lighting, color grading across all images

### Styling Requirements

#### Cleanliness & Presentation
- Vehicles should be clean, modern, well-maintained
- No visible damage, dirt, or disrepair
- Professional, aspirational aesthetic
- Reflect best-practice examples

#### Passengers & Context
- Show diverse passengers (age, ethnicity, ability)
- Include mixed-gender representation
- Families, young professionals, elderly users
- Real-world usage scenarios, not staged/sterile

#### Color Consistency
- Vehicle paint: White, silver, electric blue, or brand cyan
- Avoid overly bright or neon colors (except electric accent)
- Maintain 70% neutral tones, 30% accent colors
- Consistent color grading across all photos

#### Background Settings
- Modern urban environments (post-2015 architecture)
- Green spaces, parks, sustainable landscaping visible
- Clear skies, good natural lighting preferred
- High-quality infrastructure (clean roads, modern stops)
- Visible technology/smart city elements

### Lighting & Time of Day
- **Golden hour** (early morning, late afternoon): Warm, professional
- **Bright daylight**: Clear, modern, energetic
- **Avoid**: Night/dark settings, gloomy weather, rain
- **Consistency**: All images shot in similar lighting conditions

## 5. Typography & Text Hierarchy

### Font Selection (UN-Habitat Inspired)
**Headings**: Inter, Roboto, or Montserrat
- Bold/Semi-bold weights: 600-700
- Color: Dark Navy (#1B3A5C)
- Kerning: +0.5px for headlines

**Body Text**: Inter, Open Sans, or Source Sans Pro
- Regular weight: 400
- Color: Medium Gray (#757575)
- Line height: 1.6 (for readability)
- Font size: 16-18px

**CTAs & Buttons**: Roboto Medium
- Weight: 500
- All caps or title case
- Color: White on Cyan background

## 6. Imagery Integration Strategy

### Hero Image Best Practices
1. **Composition**: Place vehicle in power positions (rule of thirds)
2. **Depth**: Include foreground, mid-ground, background layers
3. **Movement**: Suggest direction/progress (left-to-right diagonal)
4. **Overlay Text**: Dark semi-transparent gradient (top-left to bottom-right)
5. **Safe Zone**: Keep text away from central 1/3 of image

### Carousel/Slider Images
- **Timing**: 4-5 second auto-play interval
- **Transition**: Fade or subtle slide (avoid jarring effects)
- **Mobile**: Stacked single column on phones
- **Accessibility**: Include alt-text descriptions for all images

### Background Images
- **Opacity**: Reduce image to 30-50% brightness
- **Overlay**: Add semi-transparent color overlay (navy or gradient)
- **Blur**: Optional subtle blur (3-5px) for text readability
- **File Size**: Optimize for web (< 200KB per image)

## 7. Responsive Design & Mobile Optimization

### Breakpoints
- **Desktop**: 1200px+ (full 3-column layouts)
- **Tablet**: 768px-1199px (2-column layouts)
- **Mobile**: Below 768px (single column stacking)

### Mobile Vehicle Image Strategy
- **Aspect ratios**: Use 16:9 for most vehicles
- **Cropping**: Center-focus, avoid losing vehicle details
- **Size**: Optimize full-width images (max 1000px width)
- **Touch targets**: Ensure carousel buttons are 44px+ minimum

## 9. Accessibility Standards

### Alt Text Examples
```
❌ Bad: "Bus"
✅ Good: "Modern electric bus with passengers boarding at sustainable transit station in downtown district"

❌ Bad: "Bike"
✅ Good: "Person riding electric bicycle on protected bike lane in residential neighborhood with green trees"

❌ Bad: "Car"
✅ Good: "White shared mobility vehicle parked in urban car-sharing station with digital display"
```

### Color Contrast
- All text on vehicle images must meet WCAG AA standards (4.5:1 minimum)
- Use text shadows or semi-transparent overlays if needed
- Test with color blindness simulators

### Video Alternatives
- All video content must have captions
- Vehicle demo videos: Include descriptive audio track
- Auto-play videos should be muted

## 10. Performance Optimization

### Image Optimization
```
Format: WebP with JPG fallback
- Hero images: 90-100 quality, < 300KB
- Carousel images: 85-90 quality, < 200KB
- Thumbnails: 75 quality, < 100KB

Responsive Images:
<picture>
  <source srcset="vehicle-large.webp" media="(min-width: 1200px)">
  <source srcset="vehicle-medium.webp" media="(min-width: 768px)">
  <img src="vehicle-small.jpg" alt="[descriptive text]">
</picture>
```

### Lazy Loading
- Implement for all below-fold vehicle images
- Intersection Observer API for smooth loading
- Placeholder blurred version during load

### CDN & Caching
- Use image CDN (Cloudinary, Imgix) for optimization
- Set browser cache: 1 month for static vehicle images
- Implement HTTP/2 push for above-fold images

## Conclusion

This design system provides a comprehensive framework for integrating vehicle imagery into a sustainable urban mobility website in the style of UN-Habitat. The key principles are:

1. **Professional, aspirational imagery** of modern, clean vehicles
2. **Diverse representation** of passengers and communities
3. **Clear visual hierarchy** using the cyan/navy color system
4. **Responsive, accessible design** that works on all devices
5. **Performance optimization** for fast loading worldwide
6. **Interactive elements** that engage users with vehicle data

By following these guidelines, your website will effectively communicate the vision of sustainable, multimodal urban transportation while maintaining visual consistency and brand integrity.
