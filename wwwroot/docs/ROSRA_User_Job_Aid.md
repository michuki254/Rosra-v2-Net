## 1. What is ROSRA?

ROSRA (Rapid Own-Source Revenue Analysis) is a diagnostic and planning tool developed by UN-Habitat to help local governments assess and optimize their own-source revenue (OSR). OSR includes revenue that local governments generate independently, such as:

| Revenue Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Property Tax** | Taxes levied on land and buildings |
| **Business Licenses** | Fees for operating permits and trade licenses |
| **User Charges (Short-Term)** | Daily parking fees, market stall fees |
| **User Charges (Long-Term)** | Monthly lease fees, rental income |
| **Mixed User Charges** | Combinations of daily and monthly fees |
| **Other Revenue** | Fines, penalties, revenue from government assets |

ROSRA helps you identify revenue gaps, prioritize reforms, and develop actionable recommendations to increase local government revenue.

## 5. Top-Down Approach (Quick Estimate)

The Top-Down approach provides a rapid OSR potential estimate based on aggregate data and peer benchmarking.

### 5.1 Potential Estimates Tab

1. Navigate to the **Top-Down** tab.
2. Fill in the **Project Details**:
   - **Project Name** -- Name of the revenue improvement initiative.
   - **Project Description** -- Brief summary of the project scope.
   - **Key Objectives** -- What the project aims to achieve.
   - **Start Date** and **End Date** -- Planned project timeline.
   - **Estimated Budget** -- Budget allocated for reform implementation.
3. The system automatically calculates OSR potential estimates based on:
   - Your baseline OSR and budgeted OSR figures.
   - GDP per capita and population data.
   - Peer subnational government (SNG) benchmarks from the same country and income group.
4. Review the estimated revenue potential and gap analysis summary.

### 5.2 When to Use Top-Down

- Initial scoping of revenue potential before a detailed study.
- Briefing senior officials or elected leaders on overall revenue gaps.
- Comparing your jurisdiction against peer benchmarks.
- When detailed stream-level data is not yet available.

## 6. Bottom-Up Approach (Detailed Analysis)

The Bottom-Up approach follows a structured 4-step pipeline for comprehensive revenue diagnostics and reform planning.

### Step 1: Gap Analysis (Diagnostic)

Gap Analysis is the core diagnostic step. You will enter data for each revenue stream to identify where revenue is being lost.

#### 6.1 Property Tax

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 1: Gap Analysis > Property Tax**.

Enter the following data points:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Registered Properties** | Total number of properties in the fiscal cadastre |
| **Non-Registered Properties** | Estimated number of properties not yet in the cadastre |
| **Compliant Properties** | Number of properties that paid their tax |
| **Total Fiscal Base** | Total assessed value in the fiscal cadastre |
| **Total Market Value** | Estimated total market value of all properties |
| **Billed Amount** | Total property tax amount billed |
| **Outstanding Amount** | Total property tax amount remaining unpaid |
| **Revenue to Date** | Actual property tax revenue collected |

**Automatic Calculations:**

The system computes three types of revenue gaps:

- **Coverage Gap** -- Revenue lost because properties are not registered in the cadastre. Formula: (Non-Registered / Total Properties) x Current Revenue.
- **Compliance Gap** -- Revenue lost because registered property owners do not pay. Formula: (Non-Compliant / Registered) x Billed Amount.
- **Valuation Gap** -- Revenue lost because assessed values are lower than market values. Formula: (Market Value - Fiscal Base) / Market Value x Revenue.
- **Total Functional Gap** -- Sum of all three gaps, representing total unrealized property tax revenue.

#### 6.2 Business License

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 1: Gap Analysis > Business License**.

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Registered Businesses** | Number of businesses with valid licenses |
| **Estimated Unregistered (%)** | Percentage of businesses operating without licenses |
| **Billed Amount** | Total license fees billed |
| **Outstanding Amount** | Fees billed but not collected |
| **Statutory Average Billed** | What the average license fee should be per statute |
| **Realistic Improvement (%)** | Achievable percentage improvement in billing rates |

**Automatic Calculations:**
- **Coverage Gap** -- Revenue from unregistered businesses.
- **Compliance Gap** -- Revenue from non-paying licensed businesses.
- **Liability Gap** -- Revenue from under-billing relative to statutory rates.

#### 6.3 Short-Term User Charges

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 1: Gap Analysis > Short-Term User Charges**.

Used for daily-rate revenue sources such as:
- Daily parking fees
- Market stall daily fees
- Bus terminal daily levies

Enter the number of units, daily rate, collection rate, and operating days.

#### 6.4 Long-Term User Charges

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 1: Gap Analysis > Long-Term User Charges**.

Used for monthly/annual-rate revenue sources such as:
- Monthly market stall leases
- Building permit fees
- Annual billboard advertising fees

Enter the number of units, monthly rate, and collection rate.

#### 6.5 Mixed User Charges

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 1: Gap Analysis > Mixed User Charges**.

Used when a revenue stream has both daily and monthly components.

#### 6.6 Generic Streams

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 1: Gap Analysis > Generic Streams**.

Use this tab to add **custom revenue streams** that do not fit the standard categories. Click **Add Stream** to create a new entry with your own labels and data fields. This is useful for:
- Advertising levies
- Land rental fees
- Utility surcharges
- Any locally-specific revenue source

#### 6.7 Total Estimate

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 1: Gap Analysis > Total Estimate**.

This tab provides a **consolidated summary** of all revenue gaps across all streams:
- Total Coverage Gap (all streams combined)
- Total Compliance Gap (all streams combined)
- Total Valuation/Liability Gap (all streams combined)
- **Grand Total Functional Gap** -- The total additional revenue your jurisdiction could potentially collect

---

### Step 2: Prioritization

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 2: Prioritization**.

After completing the Gap Analysis, this step helps you rank and prioritize revenue streams for reform:

1. The system displays all analyzed revenue streams ranked by total gap size (largest gap first).
2. Review the ranking. Each stream shows:
   - Stream name
   - Total gap amount
   - Dominant gap type (Coverage, Compliance, or Valuation/Liability)
3. You can **manually adjust priorities** by dragging streams to reorder them based on:
   - Political feasibility
   - Implementation cost
   - Quick-win potential
   - Institutional capacity
4. Select which streams to focus your reform efforts on.

**Tip:** Focus on 2-3 streams with the largest gaps and highest feasibility for the greatest impact.

---

### Step 3: Overview & Solutions Selection

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 3: Overview & Solutions Selection**.

This step connects your diagnostic findings to a **solutions database** of proven reform interventions:

1. For each prioritized revenue stream, the system displays relevant solutions organized by gap type:
   - **Compliance Solutions** -- Improving collection from registered/licensed entities
   - **Coverage Solutions** -- Expanding the tax/fee base to unregistered entities
   - **Valuation Solutions** (Property Tax) -- Updating property assessments
   - **Liability Solutions** (Licenses) -- Aligning fee schedules with statutory rates

2. Solutions are further categorized as:
   - **Generic Solutions** -- Applicable to any revenue stream
   - **Property Tax-Specific** -- Tailored for property tax reform
   - **Business License-Specific** -- Tailored for license reform

3. For each solution, review:
   - Description of the intervention
   - Implementation approach
   - Expected timeline
   - Resource requirements

4. **Select the solutions** you plan to implement by clicking on them. Selected solutions will be carried forward into your recommendations.

---

### Step 4: Recommendations

Navigate to **Bottom-Up > Step 4: Recommendations**.

This final step synthesizes your analysis into an actionable reform plan:

#### 4a. Problem Statement
Write a clear description of the current revenue situation, including:
- Current revenue performance vs. potential
- Key challenges and barriers
- Impact on service delivery

#### 4b. Root Causes
List the underlying causes of revenue underperformance:
- Click **Add Root Cause** to add each cause
- Examples: outdated cadastre, weak enforcement, low political will, insufficient staffing

#### 4c. Recommendation Summary
Write an overall summary of proposed reforms based on the solutions selected in Step 3.

#### 4d. Action Items
Create specific, actionable reform tasks:
- Click **Add Action Item** to create each task
- For each item, specify:
  - **Action description** -- What needs to be done
  - **Priority level** -- High, Medium, or Low
  - **Responsible party** (optional)
  - **Timeline** (optional)

## 8. Submitting a Report for Review

### 8.1 When to Submit

Submit your report when you have completed your analysis and are ready for expert review. Ensure you have:
- Filled in all report metadata (country, city, currency, financial year)
- Completed at least one revenue stream in the Gap Analysis
- Added root causes and recommendations (for Full completion level)

### 8.2 How to Submit

1. Go to your **Dashboard**.
2. Find the report card for the report you want to submit.
3. Click the **Submit** button (only available for reports with Draft status).
4. A confirmation dialog will appear. Click **Confirm** to proceed.
5. The report status changes to **Submitted**.

### 8.3 Completion Levels

When you submit, the system automatically assesses your report's completeness:

| Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Metadata** | Only location and financial information entered |
| **Partial** | At least one revenue stream has data, but analysis sections are incomplete |
| **Full** | Stream data + root causes + recommendations + prioritization all completed |

### 8.4 After Submission

- Your report appears in the **Submission Queue** for reviewers.
- You can **Withdraw** a submitted report to return it to Draft status (before a reviewer claims it).
- If a reviewer requests revision, the report returns to your dashboard with a **Needs Revision** status and a message explaining what changes are needed.
- Edit the report as requested, then **resubmit**.

## 9. The Review & Validation Process

### 9.1 Overview of Workflow

Reports follow this lifecycle:

```
Draft --> Submitted --> Under Review --> Validated
                             |
                             +--> Needs Revision --> (User edits) --> Submitted (again)
```

### 9.2 For Reviewers: Accessing the Queue

1. Navigate to the **Submission Queue** (available to users with Reviewer or Admin roles).
2. The queue has four tabs:

| Tab | Shows |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Reports submitted and waiting for a reviewer to claim |
| **My Reviews** | Reports you have claimed and are currently reviewing |
| **Revision** | Reports sent back to authors for revision |
| **All** | All non-draft reports across all statuses |

3. Use the **search bar** to find specific reports.
4. Use the **completion filter** to filter by Metadata, Partial, or Full completion level.

### 9.3 For Reviewers: Reviewing a Report

1. Click on a report in the queue to open the **Review Page**.
2. The review page shows:
   - **Full Report Preview** -- All data entered by the author, displayed in read-only format.
   - **Stream Completeness Checklist** -- Shows which sections have data (Property Tax, Licenses, User Charges, Generic Streams, Causes, Recommendations, Prioritization, Solutions).
   - **Version History** -- Previous snapshots of the report (submission versions, validation versions).
   - **Review Notes** -- Chronological thread of comments from all reviewers.

3. **Claim the Report**: Click **Claim** to assign the report to yourself for review. This changes the status to **Under Review** and prevents other reviewers from working on it simultaneously.

4. **Add Notes**: Use the notes form to add comments, feedback, or questions for the report author.

5. **Make a Decision**:
   - **Validate** -- Approve the report. Status changes to **Validated** and a validation snapshot is created.
   - **Request Revision** -- Send the report back to the author with a message explaining what needs to change. Status changes to **Needs Revision**.
   - **Unclaim** -- Release the report back to the Pending queue if you cannot complete the review.

### 9.4 For Admins: Additional Review Actions

- **Assign Reviewer** -- Assign a specific reviewer to a report.
- **Unlock Validated Report** -- Reopen a validated report for further edits if needed.

## 14. Glossary of Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **OSR** | Own-Source Revenue -- Revenue generated locally by a government, not from transfers or grants |
| **Gap Analysis** | Diagnostic assessment identifying where revenue is being lost |
| **Coverage Gap** | Revenue lost because taxable entities (properties, businesses) are not registered |
| **Compliance Gap** | Revenue lost because registered entities do not pay what they owe |
| **Valuation Gap** | Revenue lost because assessed property values are lower than market values |
| **Liability Gap** | Revenue lost because fee schedules are lower than statutory/optimal rates |
| **Functional Gap** | Total revenue gap combining all gap types for a single revenue stream |
| **Top-Down Analysis** | Quick estimation approach using aggregate data and benchmarks |
| **Bottom-Up Analysis** | Detailed diagnostic approach analyzing individual revenue streams |
| **SNG** | Subnational Government -- Any government below the national level (state, county, city, municipality) |
| **Peer SNG** | Similar subnational governments used for benchmarking comparison |
| **Fiscal Cadastre** | Official register of properties used for property tax assessment |
| **Revenue Stream** | A specific category of revenue (e.g., property tax, business licenses) |
| **Prioritization** | Ranking revenue streams by reform potential and feasibility |
| **Validation** | Formal approval of a report by an authorized reviewer |
| **Snapshot** | A saved version of the report at a specific point in time (submission or validation) |

## 15. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I work on a report over multiple sessions?**
A: Yes. Click **Save** in the left sidebar to save your progress. When you return, open the report from your Dashboard and continue where you left off. Note that unsaved changes in the current session will be lost if you close the browser without saving.

**Q: Can I edit a report after submitting it?**
A: Not directly. You must first **Withdraw** the submission (if it has not yet been claimed by a reviewer). If a reviewer has already claimed it, you will need to wait for their decision. If the reviewer requests revision, the report will return to your dashboard for editing.

**Q: What happens when a reviewer requests revision?**
A: The report status changes to **Needs Revision** and appears on your dashboard with a message from the reviewer explaining what changes are needed. Edit the report as requested, then click **Submit** again to resubmit.

**Q: Can I delete a report permanently?**
A: Regular users can soft-delete reports (move to trash). Only administrators can permanently delete reports from the Deleted Reports page.

**Q: How is the completion level determined?**
A: The system checks what sections of your report have data. **Metadata** means only location/financial info is filled. **Partial** means at least one revenue stream has data. **Full** means streams, root causes, recommendations, and prioritization are all completed.

**Q: Can I use both Top-Down and Bottom-Up approaches in the same report?**
A: Yes. Both approaches exist within the same report. You can fill in the Top-Down quick estimate and also complete the detailed Bottom-Up analysis. Switch between them using the tabs at the top of the editor.

**Q: How do I add a revenue stream that is not in the standard categories?**
A: Use the **Generic Streams** tab under Step 1: Gap Analysis. Click **Add Stream** to create a custom revenue source with your own labels and data fields.

**Q: Who can review and validate reports?**
A: Users with the **Reviewer** or **Admin** role can access the Submission Queue, claim reports, add review notes, and validate or request revision.

**Q: How do I export charts and data for presentations?**
A: Use the **Export** button in the left sidebar. Choose PDF for formatted documents with charts, or Excel for raw data tables. You can also use **Print** for a browser-based print layout.
